When I Fell In Love With EDM

Anna Liu

CS & Bio Student at Duke

I remember my first experience with electronic dance music (EDM) as if it happened yesterday. That moment has since been forever ingrained into my memory. On a hot summer’s night in San Francisco during his Freaks of Nature tour, DJ Ryan Raddon, more famously known by his stage name Kaskade, planted a seed into my heart that, over the years, has blossomed into a fervent passion for EDM.

Growing up, I was trained as a pianist in the classical style, and much to the chagrin of my music teacher, had my fair share of exposure to rock bands and pop stars. However, I found my listening experiences with most music genres to be ephemeral. Sure, the music was pleasant and appealing, but I could never truly resonate with those sounds.

Perhaps my reasons for failing to connect with most musical genres rested in the limiting sound-producing capabilities of traditional instruments utilized in making those sounds or the predictability of the composition techniques employed in organizing those sounds. I longed for a musical genre that could unequivocally capture the spectrum of human emotions through its liberating sounds, and transform the passive listening of musical performance into an unforgettable, cathartic, perhaps even metaphysical, experience.

I remember standing in the front lines of the audience, watching DJ Ryan Raddon expertly spin the turntables amidst a stunning backdrop of visuals and lights, while the audience listened and danced to the electronic dance music groove. I felt the warm embrace of each independent textural layer of the music. As my mind was soothed by atmospheric musical lines, my body effortlessly swayed to the spontaneous beat of metrical dissonance.

The musical journey of that concert was intense. At times, I felt the sharp pierce of the clashing progression of abrasive, electro-influenced synths. At other times, I was shrouded by the ethereal harmonies of mesmerizing reverberations. The peak of the performance was accompanied by an immense buildup and release of adrenaline that I experienced with the DJ’s drop, the point where after a suspenseful, musically minimalist break, the beats and the drums effectively rise and fire, causing the entire dance floor to erupt in a volcanic explosion of ecstatic dance movements.

My first encounter with electronic dance music that night was nothing short of surreal. During Kaskade’s performance, I was not just listening to the music. I was moving with the music. I was at one with myself, I was at one with the audience, and I was at one with the DJ. While dancing in the ambience of those novel electronic sounds, I could truly grasp the continually transforming musical tapestry spun by the disk-jockey on his turntables. It was an electrifying night of musical euphoria.

During my first encounter with electronic dance music, I found myself connecting with this musical genre on a physical and cognitive level in a setting characterized by an overwhelming presence of technology. There was no traditional instruments in sight, yet the presence of a rich, musical symphony blasting from sets of huge speaker stacks is undeniable. To a first timer of EDM, the possibility of a one-man orchestra seems implausible, yet up on stage stood sole DJ Ryan Raddon effortlessly manipulating a set of turntables and a complex mixing board, selecting and blending a diverse array of distinctive sounds in real time.

It is a marvel how the described technological scene of an electronic dance music performance can still generate such an exhilarating human experience. I remember the smile etched across my face as I sensed the musical meter in my body and I felt supernaturally invigorated by the energy that emanated from my fellow dancers. The collective joy experienced by the crowd was evident in our faces, and Kaskade’s fluid mastery of a continuous rhythm was manifested in the beats that were visible in the spontaneous creativity of our seemingly perpetual movements.
