On Medium and Writing

Anna Liu

CS & Bio Student at Duke

I think I got a little too excited about Medium.

I found some free time today, and began my personal product review of Medium. It is a simple, beautiful and intuitive platform, and I encourage anyone who loves to write to start publishing on the platform immediately!

Since my product review was on Medium, I thought it was only fitting to publish the review on Medium as well. Well, here goes.

Not too big, not too small. Finally a platform dedicated to writing.

I started using Medium a couple months ago. Every time I log on to this platform, I am still struck by its beauty of design and simplicity of use.

However, what I find most appealing about Medium is that it is the sole blogging platform purely dedicated to writing.

Medium has become my incentive to write. My content is published on a gorgeous, minimalist user interface. In addition, medium’s machine learning algorithms help my writing reach the most suitable audience.

Gone are the days when I spent hours on end selecting the appropriate typeface combinations to format my posts. Gone are the days when I experimented with keywords and search engine optimization to attract a targeted audience.

Now I can simply focus on writing and improving the quality of my prose.

So why do I write?

I write because I have a spontaneous, curious mind.

Writing is a tool for me to voice my opinions and contemplate about pertinent issues.

It helps me to analyze the perspectives of others and develop new insights on controversial topics. It enables me to vividly connect incoherent thoughts and provides me with vast opportunities to improvise and create.

I write because writing stimulates my curiosity, helping me to identify research interests, to explore topics of interest, and to formulate new questions.

I write because writing reminds me that I am human, guiding me to reflect upon my social experiences and to learn from my past mistakes.

Coupled with an abundance of technology that enables the rapid proliferation of ideas and information, I believe that my voice gains greater influence the more I write.

Medium is a piece of technology that pushes for the power of the written word in modern society. Novice writers like me can easily publish on this platform and have access to a plethora of high-quality works to read and learn from.

Technology, indeed, has made the written word accessible at the touch of a fingertip. The more coherent, the more eloquent, the more persuasive a piece of writing is, the more likely its message will leave an imprint in the mind of its reader.

Writing is necessary for communication and expression. It’s potential is limitless.

It provides the individual with an ability to establish a public image, forge a new identity, and escape into the deepest crevices of one’s imagination.

Writing can be used to expand, manipulate, and inspire thought.

It enables a person to tell a story, remember the details of a recent event, and pass down precious knowledge from generation to generation.

Writing is an art, and when properly mastered, serves as a powerful tool to influence the masses.

With that said about writing, I am inspired by Medium’s dedication to the sharing of ideas and experiences that move humanity forward.

Its minimalist design enables the writer to focus on his or her words. Its user interface provides a collaborative setting for the writer to write with other people.

And most importantly, it is the disruptive technology that enables the even most novice of writers to find their audience and share what they have to say about the world.
