On Writing

Anna Liu

Graduated from Duke. Moved to Silicon Valley. Forever a blue devil.

The following is adapted from a series of short papers that I wrote for a class on Greek Civilizations as a first-year undergraduate.

Why the written word?

Writing is necessary for communication and expression. It provides the individual with an ability to establish a public image, forge a new identity, and escape into the deepest crevices of one’s imagination. Writing can be used to expand, manipulate, and inspire thought. It enables a person to tell a story, remember the details of a recent event, and pass down precious knowledge from generation to generation. Writing is an art, and when properly mastered, serves as a powerful tool to influence the masses and the outcome of history.

In the modern era, the written word is prolific. Online journals, electronic novels, digital newspapers, discussion forums, personal blogs, etc., etc., are abound on the Internet. Technology, indeed, has made the written word accessible at the touch of a fingertip. The more coherent, the more eloquent, the more persuasive a piece of writing is, the more likely its message will leave an imprint in the mind of its reader.

Why do I write?

In high school, writing was presented to me as formal, stringent, and orthodox. Experimentation with novel, aesthetic techniques was frowned upon by English teachers even if the logic of an essay was sound. Writing is an art, and no limits can curb the potential of an art.

For the most part, I consider myself to possess an objective, linear mindset, but writing is my artistic outlet. I discovered my passion for the written word through writing song lyrics because it has taught me to explore the many layers and meanings of a single word. Take, for example, sadness. In music, literary devices can be utilized to express sadness from its tamest to wildest representations. Sadness may simply be tears flowing down one’s cheeks, or repeated cycles of drowning out one’s never-ending sorrows. I attempt to string words into idiosyncratic phrases in order to give my lyrics a unique touch.

Writing is a tool for me to voice my opinions and contemplate about pertinent issues. It helps me to analyze the perspectives of others and develop new insights on controversial topics. It enables me to vividly connect incoherent thoughts and provides me with vast opportunities to improvise and create.
