Igor Stravinsky's Symphony of Psalms

Anna Liu

CS & Bio Student at Duke

In his Symphony of Psalms, Stravinsky’s juxtaposition of a powerful chorus with the orchestral sounds of wind instruments, pianos, harp and low strings instills a heavy spiritual feeling in the hearts of its listeners. The composer’s use of neoclassical techniques enhances the religious nature of David’s Psalms as expressed in the Latin Vulgate. Stravinsky is successful in his attempts to symphonize the singing of the Psalms through the incorporation of fugue elements reminiscent of the Renaissance and Baroque eras. In contrast to his claims that “music is, by its very nature, essentially powerless to express anything at all,” his piece effortlessly captures the ritual atmosphere of intense and passionate worship, and sensually portrays the buildup and release of spiritual catharsis, through a palette of tonal colors and thundering voices.
