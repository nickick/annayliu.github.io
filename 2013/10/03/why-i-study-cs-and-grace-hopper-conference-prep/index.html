Why I Study Computer Science

Anna Liu

CS & Bio Student at Duke

This post was originally published on Duke at the 2013 Grace Hopper Celebration found here

I am very excited to attend the Grace Hopper Conference this year. I have gone to technology conferences in the past, but never to one at GHC's scale and one especially dedicated to women in computer science. I am extremely grateful for Duke University's computer science department for providing me with such an opportunity, and I cannot wait to not only share this amazing experience with my Duke peers and professors, but also meet and network with successful women in academia, research, and technology.

At Duke, I study computer science and biology, and I will be graduating this upcoming May 2014. In terms of my academic career, much has changed for me since I started college. I originally entered Duke as a biomedical engineering major in Pratt hoping to pursue a research career in the biotechnology industry. However, I realized that my personality was not suited for research; it was not as fast-paced as I had anticipated, and I found the long, late hours that came with preparing biological specimen to be unappealing. After freshman year, I switched to Trinity to become a biology major and considered the pre-medical track throughout my sophomore year. However, it was not until junior year when I discovered that computer science and technology fully satisfied my intellectual curiosities.

Some reasons that I decided to study computer science at Duke:

  • Computer science is a rapidly-evolving field, and technological progress increases at an exponential rate. I  want to be able to contribute to that progress.

  • Algorithms and theories in computer science can be applied to problems in any domain. I want to possess the ability to solve challenging problems in the domains that appeal to me.

  • I have found that the technical and engineering skills that I have developed from computer science can easily be applied to other disciplines.

After I graduate from Duke, I hope to pursue a technology career in Silicon Valley. I hope that the Grace Hopper Conference will be a stepping stone in launching my career. I leave for Minneapolis this coming Tuesday. I am excited for the opportunities that I will encounter at the GHC in the next few days.
