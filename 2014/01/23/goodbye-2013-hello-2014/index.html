Goodbye, 2013. Hello, 2014.

Anna Liu

Graduated from Duke. Moved to Silicon Valley. Forever a blue devil.

2013 was an extremely interesting year.

In 2013, I lost and found myself. On January, 1, 2013, in the aftermath of an euphoric night dancing to the electronic sounds of disk-jockey Dash Berlin, without even realizing,  a part of me plummeted deep within, buried alive in the deepest crevices of my mind. Sometime during the middle of 2013, I journeyed to that deep crevice, and discovered my greatest fear: empty happiness. The rest of 2013 was a journey to not only escape but conquer that fear, to fix the broken pieces and become at one with myself again.

In 2013, I fell in love with what I had missed growing up in the San Francisco Bay Area. My passion dangled like a carrot in front of my eyes, but I was too blind to see it. In the past four years, I flew across the country too many times and explored countless subjects, but my endeavors were not conducted in vain. There is a reason that the turtle, and not the rabbit won the race. My gut feelings told me that each step would gradually take me to my final destination. Now, all I can say is that when you finally know what you want out of life, you truly understand what 20/20 foresight and hindsight vision feels like. Looking backwards, everything I did made sense. Looking forwards, I am ready to tackle what I have set my heart out to do.

In 2013, I became obsessed with technology and electronic dance music. I am addicted to TechCrunch, Fast Company, Kaskade, TyDi, Crunchbase, Wired, Dash Berlin, VentureBeat, Mashable, Dada Life, etc. No tech, no EDM for 24 hours, my brain goes into an immediate state of withdrawal. Innovation, creativity, and ideas in the form of technology and electronic dance music spark my drive. Thus it makes sense that if the source is removed, I wither.

In 2013, my twenty-one years of linear thinking has been shattered. Life does not consist of discrete units. It is a continuous, unpredictable flow. For the first time in my life, I am content with not knowing what the future holds. By changing my mindset, I felt a heavy weight lifted off my shoulders. This does not mean that I have decided to live a life of complete spontaneity. Instead, I tune out distractions and direct my life to encounter serendipity.

At the end of 2013, as the clock was about to strike midnight,  I was calmed by the realization that I have conquered everything life threw at me in the past 365 days.

As 2013 ends, and 2014 begins, I begin the rest of my life.
