Hack the Mind By Learning

Anna Liu

Graduating from Duke University. Moving on to tech paradise in Silicon Valley. Forever a blue devil.

Hack your mind by living, laughing, learning, and loving.

Learning.

Seriously, learning is the best way to not only exercise your brain but also a chance for you to invest in yourself.

Education will enable you to:

  • Become more aware of what goes on in the world
  • Discover and develop your passion
  • Provide you with the tools and strategies to help you get ahead of the game

College has been my academic playground. Since day one at Duke, I made it my goal to discover what I would be truly passionate about.

Finding out what I wanted to do with my life was no easy task. I considered at least over ten majors (from biomedical engineering to classical studies), took classes in a broad array of subjects, and explored many potential career paths.

Along the way, I made mistakes, but I learned more about myself with each new educational opportunity that I pursued.

Although I decided to declare a double major in biology and computer science near the end of my sophomore year, I didn't know what I wanted to do this degree until the first semester of my senior year.

Last semester, I decided to take a break from my extracurricular activities and invest my time learning about startups and companies in Silicon Valley. By doing so, I realized that there was more to the tech industry than just being a software developer.

I caught the startup bug. I discovered a new frontier of creativity and innovation that lay in the heart of my most favorite city in the world: San Francisco.

I love to code and I love to build. I have greater ambitions than just coding and building. I want to learn how to channel my creativity to impact the world. And I want to learn how to utilize my passion for technology to create innovative products for people to lead healthy, productive, and exciting lives.

I don't know where life will take me, but I know that all the learning opportunities that I pursued at Duke have brought me to where I am today. I want to create and innovate, and my studies at Duke have provided me with the skills and toolset to do so.

Near the conclusion of my college career, I have finally discovered what I want to do with my life. And I will make learning my lifelong endeavor to turn my ultimate dreams and aspirations into reality.

I once received an email from a professor at Duke who wrote that she learned best by investing in her time and money. I agree wholeheartedly. Attending Duke University and pursuing two majors in the STEM fields are two of the best decisions I have made in my life.

I will keep on learning. I will continue to put my learning to good use. I will continue to invest myself through learning.

Put your heart and soul into your endeavors, and they will bear you fruitful results.
